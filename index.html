Welcome to My Research Page

I'm a PhD student at UC Berkeley in the Physics department, working under the supervision of Victoria Xu. My research focuses on reducing quantum noise in the LIGO experiment with particular emphasis on squeezed light generation.

Research Interests

Quantum Noise & Precision Measurement

I am interested in testing fundamental physics at the interface of quantum mechanics and gravity. Tabletop experiments probing gravity & fundamental constants offer an exciting chance to design and build something from the ground up.

LIGO: Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory

I am part of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration at Berkeley, working on developing technologies to enable higher levels of squeezing to reduce the impact of quantum noise on gravitational wave detecton.

Outreach Activities

I served as a Math and Physics Sciences mentor with a goal of supporting undergraduate students' aspirations to pursue careers in STEM. This program aims to give all students equal access to mentorship, support, and rescources essential to graduate school applications and career advancement.

Teaching & Mentorship

I have taught Physcs 7A: Introductory Mechanics for Engineers, and Physics 10: Descriptive introduction to Quantum Technologies. I developed course materials such as lecture slides, quiz questions, discussion worksheets, and assisted in developing the content for the Quantum Computing half of the course.

Gaussian Beam Collimation
October 2025

This notebok calculates the right focal length of an aspheric lens to collimate a beam given a desired beam diameter and known beam divergence of the light being emitted from a fiber to free space. Beam divergence can be calculated if one knows the numerical aperature of the fiber.

Topics:Gaussian Beam Analysis

Mode Matching Tool
October 2025

The mode matching notebook uses an inputted beam profile to fit waists, then optimizes the positions of optical lenses to maximize the coupling efficiency (mode overlap) between the beam and a desired target beam at a specific location. It provides an interactive GUI able to configure lenses with optimization bounds, and visualize the resulting beam propagation and Gouy phase through an optical system.
