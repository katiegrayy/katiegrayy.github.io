Katie Gray is a Ph.D. student in Physics at the University of California, Berkeley, working with Dr. Victoria Xu. Her research interests span quantum metrology, gravitational wave detection, and atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) physics, with emphasis on quantum noise reduction techniques for precision measurements.

My current work focuses on using cavity qed to explore new geometries for improved squeezing in optical parametric amplifiers. I am also interested in developing tools and simulations that help understand quantum noise in precision measurement systems.

I received my B.S. in Physics from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in 2023. Before beginning grad school, I spent several months working as an au pair in Bayreuth, Germany, where I spent my time exploring the city and developing a love for opera.

Current Projects

Interactive Squeeze @ LIGO
Developing an interactive GUI to visualize and analyze noise spectra and frequency-dependent squeezing in the LIGO detectors. This work includes modeling optical loss, phase noise, and detector parameters to optimize detector sensitivity.
Optical Parametric Amplifier Simulations
Investigating the behavior of fields in cavities with complex geometry, how to represent losses, and optimizing cavity parameters to maximize squeezed light generation.

Optical Systems & Gaussian Beam Analysis

Gaussian Beam Collimation
October 2025

This notebook calculates the optimal focal length of an aspheric lens to collimate a beam given a desired beam diameter and known beam divergence. The tool accounts for fiber numerical aperture and provides complete beam propagation analysis.

Topics: Gaussian beam propagation, optical design, fiber optics

[Preview] [Open in Colab]

Mode Matching Optimization Tool
October 2025

An interactive tool for optimizing lens positions to maximize coupling efficiency between Gaussian beams. Features include beam waist fitting, multi-lens optimization with configurable bounds, and visualization of beam propagation and Gouy phase evolution through optical systems.

Topics: Mode matching, optical coupling, beam propagation, optimization algorithms
